Women And Gore

The Halloween Version of Women Who Roared!

Shirley Jackson

Author

Jackson was an American writer, known primarily for her works of horror and mystery. She gained significant public attention for her short story "The Lottery", which details a sinister underside to a bucolic American village. In 1959, she published The Haunting of Hill House, a supernatural horror novel widely considered to be one of the best ghost stories ever written.

Jennifer Kent

Director

Jennifer Kent is an Australian actress, writer and director, best known for her critically acclaimed directorial debut, the horror film The Babadook (2014). The film focuses on a single mother and her child and their experience with an eerie children's book titled "Mister Babadook".

Mary Shelley

Author

Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley (1797 – 1851) was an English novelist who wrote the Gothic novel Frankenstein; or the Modern Prometheus. The book has had a considerable influence in literature and popular culture and spawned a complete genre of horror stories, films and plays.

Elizabeth Báthory

Countess

Countess Elizabeth Báthory de Ecsed (August 7, 1560 – August 21, 1614) was a Hungarian noblewoman. She has been labeled by Guinness World Records as the most prolific female murderer, although the precise number of her victims is debated. Some insist she inspired Bram Stoker's Dracula (1897), though there is no evidence to support this hypothesis. Nicknames and literary epithets attributed to her include The Blood Countess and Countess Dracula.

Cassandra Peterson

Actress

Cassandra Peterson (born September 17, 1951) is an American actress, writer and singer. She is best known for her portrayal of the horror hostess character Elvira, Mistress of the Dark. Peterson gained fame on Los Angeles television station KHJ-TV wearing a revealing, black, gothic, cleavage-enhancing gown as host of Elvira's Movie Macabre, a weekly B movie presentation.

Siouxsie Sioux

Singer/Songwriter

Susan Janet Ballion, known professionally as Siouxsie Sioux (born May 27, 1957), is an English singer, songwriter, musician, and record producer. She is best known as the lead singer of the goth rock band Siouxsie and the Banshees (1976–1996). Much of their music is in keeping with the Halloween spirit, especially the songs "Halloween" and "Trick or Treat"!

Barb Balogh

Creator of Boo Buddies

Barb is Service Unit Director for the Lakewood Girl Scouts. Barb's known for spearheading new events for Girl Scouts to enjoy, such as Women Who Roared and the Firefly Formal. Her latest inspiration, Boo Buddies, is currently spreading the joy of Halloween throughout Lakewood and beyond!

Edna Sullivan Lanchester

Actress

Elsa Sullivan Lanchester (October 28, 1902 – December 26, 1986) was an English actress with a long career in theatre, film and television. She played the Monster's Bride in Bride of Frankenstein. Her conical hairdo and white lightning-trace steaks on each side, has become an iconic symbol of the character.

Lizzie Borden

Murder Suspect

Lizzie Andrew Borden (July 19, 1860 – June 1, 1927) was an American woman who was the main suspect in the August 4, 1892 axe murders of her father and stepmother in Fall River, Massachusetts. Borden was tried and acquitted of the murders. The case received widespread newspaper coverage throughout the United States. Borden's association with the murders has remained a topic in American popular culture, and she has been depicted in various films, theatrical productions, and literary works.

Anne Rice

Author

Anne Rice (born Howard Allen Frances O'Brien, October 4, 1941) is an American author. She is best known for her series of novels The Vampire Chronicles, which revolve around the central character Lestat. Two of her books were the subject of film adaptations, Interview with the Vampire (1994), and Queen of the Damned (2002).

Ruth Edna Kelley

Author

Ruth Edna Kelley (April 8, 1893 – March 4, 1982) was an American librarian and author. She is chiefly remembered for The Book of Hallowe'en (1919), the first book-length history of the holiday.

Carolyn Sue Jones

Actress

Carolyn Sue Jones (April 28, 1930 – August 3, 1983) was an American actress of television and film. She is known for the role of Morticia Addams in the original black and white television series The Addams Family.
